Chapter One

Me, Holistically

A full-circle look at who I am — from the body I live in, to the values I hold, to the identity I carry online and offline.

🌿
Physical Self
Body Image & Self-Esteem
My physical self is a work in progress — and that's something I've learned to embrace. I'm not defined by how I look but by how I carry myself. Building confidence means accepting the skin I'm in while striving to stay healthy and active. My body is the vessel for everything I want to achieve as a future IT professional.
💼
Material Self
Possessions & Lifestyle
I live simply. My most prized possessions are my laptop, my books, and my commitment to continuous learning. Material success isn't my endgame — but I understand its role in providing stability and freedom to pursue what truly matters to me. I value experiences over things.
🙏
Spiritual Self
Beliefs, Meanings & Values
Faith grounds me. Beyond religion, my spirituality is found in gratitude, purpose, and the belief that every challenge is a lesson in disguise. My values — honesty, service, and growth — guide every decision I make, from how I treat others to how I approach my studies.
❤️
Sexual Self
Love Language & Relationships
My love language is Acts of Service. I show care through action — helping, being present, and contributing. In relationships, I value depth over surface. I believe understanding yourself emotionally is the foundation for building genuine connections with others.
💻
Digital Self
Who Am I Online vs. Offline?
Online, I am a learner — browsing documentation, watching tutorials, joining developer communities. I curate a digital presence that reflects my professional aspirations. Offline, I am more reserved, more reflective. The gap between the two is something I am consciously bridging: I want my online identity to be as authentic as the person I am when no one is watching. In an age where digital footprints define reputations, I take my digital self seriously — because it is as real as any other part of me.

Epilogue

A Letter to My Present Self

Dear Khian,


I'm writing to you from the version of yourself you're still becoming. I know the days feel heavy sometimes — the deadlines stack up, the self-doubt creeps in, and it seems easier to scroll than to study. But I need you to hear this: you are more capable than you currently believe.


You chose IT not just for a career, but because you genuinely believe that technology can make life better for people. Don't lose that "why." It will carry you through the all-nighters, the failed outputs, and the moments when everyone else seems to be moving faster than you.


Your physical health matters. Your spiritual life matters. The people you love matter. No version of success that costs you these things is actually success. Work hard, yes — but also rest, pray, laugh, and live.


Version 2.0 isn't a destination. It's a direction. Keep walking. I'll see you there.


With hope and belief,
Khian G. De Vera
